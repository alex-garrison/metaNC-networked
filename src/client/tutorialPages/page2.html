Game Rules

Ultimate Noughts and Crosses is played on a large 9x9 grid, which consists of nine smaller 3x3 grids. Here are some more rules to follow:

  • Alternate Turns: Players take turns to make moves. Player 1 usually starts with noughts, and Player 2 with crosses.
  • Forced Grid: The cell you select in a smaller grid determines which smaller grid your opponent must play in next. They must make a move in the corresponding grid.
  • Winning the Game: To win the overall game, you need to win three smaller grids in a row, column, or diagonal, just like in regular Noughts and Crosses.
